✅ Fact-Checking

Test every claim
before you trust it

Prompts for verifying claims, finding counterarguments, stress-testing ideas, and separating fact from spin.

01
Fact-check this claim: [paste claim]. Tell me: is it accurate, partially accurate, misleading, or false? What does the evidence actually show? Are there important caveats or context being left out? What would someone need to know to evaluate this claim properly?
02
I'm about to share this statistic: [paste stat with source]. Before I use it, verify it for me. Is the source credible? Is the number being used correctly and in the right context? Has it been misrepresented or taken out of context? What are the limitations of this data?
03
Steelman this argument for me, then find its weakest points: [paste argument]. First, give me the strongest possible version of this argument. Then identify the 3 most significant logical flaws, unsupported assumptions, or missing evidence. Rate the overall quality of the reasoning from 1-10.
04
Here is something I read online: [paste content]. Help me evaluate the credibility of this. What signals suggest it's reliable or unreliable? What claims are verifiable vs. unverifiable? What would I need to look up to confirm or refute the key points? What's the most questionable thing in here?
05
I'm writing about [topic] and want to make sure I'm not repeating myths. What are the most common misconceptions or oversimplifications people believe about [topic]? For each myth, explain what people think is true, what's actually true, and why the myth persists.
06
Find the counterarguments to this position: [paste your position or argument]. Give me the 4-5 strongest objections someone could raise — not strawmen, but the best version of the opposing case. Then tell me which counterargument is hardest to refute and why.
07
I've heard conflicting things about [topic]. Some people say [claim A], others say [claim B]. What does the actual evidence show? Is there a genuine expert disagreement here or is one side clearly more supported? Help me understand where the real uncertainty lies.
08
Help me check the logical structure of this argument: [paste argument]. Identify any logical fallacies present, any unsupported leaps in reasoning, any places where correlation is being treated as causation, and any places where the conclusion doesn't follow from the premises. Be specific.
