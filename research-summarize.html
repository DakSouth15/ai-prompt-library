Prompts that turn long articles, papers, and documents into crisp, useful summaries in seconds.

Summarize this article in 3 sentences or less. Focus only on the single most important takeaway, why it matters, and what action (if any) someone should take because of it. Cut everything else. Here is the article: [paste article]
02
Read this research paper and give me: (1) the core argument in one sentence, (2) the key evidence or findings in 3-5 bullet points, (3) the limitations the authors acknowledge, and (4) why this matters to someone working in [your field]. Here is the paper: [paste or describe paper]
03
I have a long document that I need to digest quickly. Summarize it at three levels: (1) a 1-sentence TL;DR, (2) a 1-paragraph executive summary, and (3) a structured breakdown of the main sections with 2-3 bullet points each. Here is the document: [paste document]
04
Summarize this content as if you're explaining it to a smart 15-year-old who has no background in the topic. Use plain language, no jargon, and focus on making the core idea genuinely understandable. End with one analogy that makes it click. Here is the content: [paste content]
05
I need to summarize a meeting transcript for people who weren't there. Extract: the key decisions made, action items with owners and deadlines, unresolved questions, and any important context. Format it as a clean meeting recap email I can send directly. Here is the transcript: [paste transcript]
06
Summarize this book / report / whitepaper into a one-page brief. Structure it as: Overview (2-3 sentences), Key Arguments (3-5 points), Important Data or Evidence, Counterarguments the author addresses, and My Recommended Takeaway. Make it dense with insight, not fluff. Here is the content: [paste or describe]
